Introduction Paragraph Practice

Hook: Imagine waking up after a full night’s sleep, eating breakfast without rushing, and arriving at school feeling alert and ready to learn.

Select the most logical next sentence to continue an academic introduction:

A. Teenagers are just lazy in the mornings, so starting school later would not fix anything.
B. Across the United States, many educators and parents have debated whether high schools should start later to better align with teenagers’ natural sleep cycles.
C. Waking up early is a tradition, and everyone should simply adapt to it.
D. Most students would definitely prefer to stay in bed longer, which is not surprising.

Context Sentence: Across the United States, many educators and parents have debated whether high schools should start later to better align with teenagers’ natural sleep cycles.

Which sentence best adds background information?

A. Research in sleep science shows that teenagers’ biological clocks naturally shift toward later bedtimes during adolescence, making early start times misaligned with optimal learning.
B. Most teens stay up late because they like using their phones or playing games.
C. School start times are random and have no effect on students.
D. Teachers also like sleeping, so this issue affects everyone equally.

Background Sentence: Research in sleep science shows that teenagers’ biological clocks naturally shift toward later bedtimes during adolescence, making early start times misaligned with optimal learning.

Which sentence most effectively presents a clear thesis statement?

A. Schools should just let students pick whatever start time they want.
B. While adjusting schedules may seem challenging, the benefits of later start times for student health and performance outweigh the logistical difficulties.
C. Starting later sounds nice, but it would be too hard for buses and teachers.
D. Waking up early is part of life, and students should learn to deal with it.

🎉 Great work!

You’ve successfully built a complete academic introduction paragraph:

Imagine waking up after a full night’s sleep, eating breakfast without rushing, and arriving at school feeling alert and ready to learn. Across the United States, many educators and parents have debated whether high schools should start later to better align with teenagers’ natural sleep cycles. Research in sleep science shows that teenagers’ biological clocks naturally shift toward later bedtimes during adolescence, making early start times misaligned with optimal learning. While adjusting schedules may seem challenging, the benefits of later start times for student health and performance outweigh the logistical difficulties.

✅ Notice how each part (hook → context → background → thesis) builds logically toward a focused argument.
